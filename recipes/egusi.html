Egusi Soup

The egusi soup is a West AAfrican soup, majorly cooked by the people of Nigeria. it is a great soup for those who want to try something different.

It is best enjoyed with pounded yam, but can be eating with any swallow of your choice.

Ingredient

  • ¾ Cup Egusi seeds
  • 1½ Pounds cubed beef stew meat
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ Cup palm oil
  • 1 Small onion, chopped
  • Enough peppers (to your taste)
  • 1 ½ Cups water
  • 2 Pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 Pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • Maggi

Steps

  1. Put egusi seeds in a blender; blend until mixture is powdery. set aside.
  2. Put onions, pepper in a blender and blend until smooth.
  3. cut beef into bite-sized cubes; seasoned with salt, maggi and parboiled with medium heat.
  4. Turn egusi powder into paste by mixing egusi powder with small water
  5. Place pot on fire and pour palm oil into the pot; allow it boil for 30 to 60 seconds
  6. Add small balls of egusi paste into the oil and allow to fry with medium heat for like 5 minutes
  7. Add the mixture of the blended onions and pepper into the pot. Add small water and allow to simmer for 20 minutes
  8. Add salt and maggi to your taste
  9. Add meat and shrimp into the pot and allow to simmer for 10 minutes
  10. Add the fresh spinach, into the pot and allow to simmer for 10 minutes
